Q5. a. What properties characterize Executive Information Systems (EIS)? b. Read the article on EIS, and explain why EIS dashboards are appropriate for the CEO’s. c. From the same article, explain how Verizon is using dashboards, containing what information. d. How can you use EIS dashboards in your business? What variables would you include?
A5. a. An Executive Information System is characterized by text inputs to a database which are then processed into statistics and graphs dynamically. An EIS displays small chunks of aggregated and summarized information that can be dynamically updated using repeating queries to a main database.

b. EIS Dashboards are appropriate for Chief Executive Officers because it brings the basic details about the operations of the company to their fingertips. It also allows them to drill down on any particular statistic and make decisions based on the trends shown.

c. Verizon’s CEO Ivan Seidenberg can choose from over 300 metrics to put on his dashboard. Some options are broadband sales and wireless subscription defectors.

d. In the business of Technical Consulting, some useful metrics to include on a dashboard are: Number of customers per service, number of hours of work per location, and revenue per service. Monitoring the metrics above can give insight into which services are performing and how well. An EIS System can be used in this field in order to monitor service popularity and service center incomes so that the CEO can evaluate whether any particular service or service center should be maintained and what the corresponding prices should be.

Q6. Browse an e-book at http://library.books24x7.com or at http://www.library.cornell.edu, or at http://www.globusz.com. a. Briefly describe what book you browsed. Ingram executives believe that digital distribution of books is more important than online reading. Describe how book distributors like Ingram can accomplish digital distribution, without going into e-book retail business. c. Why is Ingram not moving more aggressively into digital distribution of physical books? Could channel conflict be one of their concerns? Explain.
A6. a. The e-Book browsed was Case in Point on a Kindle App on an Android phone and Google Chrome on a PC. Given the educational and employment related nature of the book, the inclusion of case interview videos would enhance the quality of reading. Being able to watch the conduct of an interviewee in a case interview situation would add more to the tips than simply reading about the suggested behavior. One step further, adding interactive responses to practice questions would allow the reader to practice case questions with a computer programmed to supply the interviewer responses for a realistic experience.

b. Book distributors can move into the digital distribution with the help of machines such as Espresso’s Print-on-demand machines. With such technology locally available, book distributors can electronically ship books and manuscripts to be printed on demand. This saves them the cost of holding inventory and shipping books. This is different from going into the e-book retail business because the books distributed are physical products. Only the delivery method is handled electronically.

c. Yes, Channel Conflict is one of the reasons that Ingram books is not aggressively pursuing digital distribution. Since Ingram is a book distributor, it has two channels of income: bookstores (physical products) and digital distribution. In this case, one of the income channels (physical bookstores) is threatened by the increase in popularity of the other. For this reason, physical stores have threatened to withhold business from Ingram books if they pursue espresso printing machines.

Q7. a. Can sharing of a digital product by consumers be beneficial to the sellers of that product? List your reasons, and briefly explain. b. Read the article titled “Disintermediating Cable TV”, and explain how the comedian Louis C.K. is bypassing the TV networks. Explain if any of the reasons you gave in part a apply to him. c. Can site licenses benefit software makers? Give one reason and briefly explain. d. Explain if you would encourage sharing of your products in your business and why. If you do not have digital products, give an example of another business where sharing of a product by the consumers may benefit the seller and explain why.
A7. a. Yes it can. Sharing of digital products by consumers can propagate positive network effects for that product. As more people gain access to the digital product, the popularity will grow and so will its value. This is particularly true if the product is priced way below the reservation price for most customers. The convenience of digital delivery and download at a low cost will eventually overcome the convenience sharing with other consumers.

b. The comedian Louis C. K. bypasses most intermediaries in his “supply chain”, i.e. he writes, directs, films and composes his comedy acts all by himself. Furthermore, by supplying his own acts for direct download from his website, he is bypassing TV networks and reaching consumers by himself.

c. Site licenses benefit the makers of the software by allowing them to charge more per license. Since a site license by definition allows multiple users, the reservation price of each individual buyer (i.e. site of organization) increases greatly. In most cases selling a site license will bring in more money for ‘x’ number of people than an individual license for each of those ‘x’.

d. Based on personal experience, the sharing of kindle books (digital products) would enhance business for the seller, and the experience for the reader. Amazon Kindle books were introduced to me through a sampler for a book I was waiting for. Since the format of the e-book was specific ( amazon kindle), it becomes necessary for me to download the compatible software. Once I had experience using the kindle for free, I was more inclined to pay for the digital products than before sharing. Essentially, sharing of digital media can propagate positive network effects for the digital media.
